    • What is MRSA?

    Methicillin- Resistant Staphylococcus aureus, otherwise known as MRSA, is a contagious disease requiring treatment from medical professionals. MRSA is such a hard disease to cure because it is resistant to many antibiotics. Patients in hospitals may get infected with MRSA from tools that were not cleaned properly or doctors that don’t wash their hands well; you can also get MRSA from touching a person with MRSA or touching something that person touched.

    What does it look like?

    If you get MRSA you will usually see:

    -red bumps under the skin that can be painful -swollen cuts that are filled with hot pus -Blisters with pus inside -Spider bite looking sores

    How to avoid it?

    There are many things people can do to make sure they stay as healthy as possible and to keep from spreading any other germs or diseases around. Some of them are:

    Washing your hands

    Washing your hands is one of the most important things you can do to stop the spread of any disease. Using plain old soap and water might not seem like a lot but a little goes a long way. Washing your hands for about 30 seconds -which is just as long as it takes to sing happy birthday or your ABCs- is a great way to kill germs.

    Personal hygiene

    Taking care of your body is a good thing to do to stay clean. Just taking baths and showers regularly and keeping on clean clothes is a good way to stay clean it reduces the risk of spreading infection. Not sharing razors and other toiletries are good ways to reduce the spread of MRSA.

    Using clean rags and towels

    Washing your towels and rags often (especially after visitors use them) is another way to reduce the spread of infection. Regular washes helps keep bacteria off of rags and towels. In addition to that, not sharing rags and towels with others will also help prevent infection.

    Cleaning and managing sores

    Properly cleaning cuts and sores is a great way to not get infected. Open cuts and sores can easily get infected so be sure to properly clean and cover them up. Washing your hands after you do so is also important. Do not touch sores and cuts, if this happens wash your hands.

    Using cleaning products

    Using cleaning products on countertops, in bathrooms, and in kitchens are great ways to kill bacteria. Cleaning often helps kill bacteria and lowers infection rates.

    What to do if you get infected?

    MRSA is easy to get for it is very contagious. If you do get infected tell your doctor right way so that they can go about treatment the right way. Even if you suspect you have it or are showing any of the symptoms tell a medical professional right away to get tested. Noticing the signs and staying on top of your health is the best way to prevent infections.
